• If multiple teams/products are presenting at a Product review meeting, please try to go last. Stakeholders will be tired and less likely to ask difficult questions.
• If a remote meeting is going nowhere and you have no input to offer, do a British exit. You probably have better things to do.
• If someone offers to help, take it! You probably have too much work as it is.
• If you need to cancel a meeting, inform your peers in the chat, but keep the meeting on the calendar - you will have some time for actual work.
• Book time in your calendar for lunch and focus work - spending 8h on meetings in a day is probably not productive and I'm pretty sure is a crime against humanity.
• Sometimes dire issues will resolve themselves if ignored for some time. However, it's a craft to tell things that only appear dire from those that are actually ticking time bombs. Proceed with caution!
• (Probably) Every Product Manager ever has no idea what she/he is doing. Don't be afraid to make it all up as you go along, project confidence on the way, and if it works out, let stakeholders believe it went exactly how you planned it from the start.
• (Almost) all metric impact estimations, that you provide with your proposed product updates, are GUESStimations. Everyone knows that. Don't be shy to give your best (erm... educated?) guess!
• Being silent for a long time in a room full of different opinions will allow you to speak last. You will have the benefit of knowing the full picture and appearing as a leader who speaks the wisest.
• If you don't know how to answer a question, kick the can down the road: "Let me check my data after the meeting and get back to you". Most likely the person asking the question will forget all about it once the meeting ends.
• While AI and ChatGPT are not a solution producing reliable answers to any query, give them a go. Maybe AI will give you just the numbers/research you need. Fact-check it though, so far at least the Bing bot is super unreliable for PM stuff for me. 
• Have a healthy emotional detachment from your team and product. Being stressed and carrying too much will drain you, making you a worse Product Manager.
