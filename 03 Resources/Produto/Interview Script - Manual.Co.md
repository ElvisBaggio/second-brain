## Academic Background and Continuous Learning
I am very excited about the opportunity to contribute to Manual.co. My degree in Geography from the University of São Paulo provided a solid foundation in logistics and geoprocessing, which have been fundamental in shaping my professional journey. I have always sought to improve my skills through mentoring programmes, such as the CPO Mentoring, as well as courses like design thinking and product leadership. I believe that continuous learning is essential to face the challenges of the technology and digital health sectors.

## Experience at Geograph
At **Geograph**, where I worked for 11 years, I developed extensive experience in geoprocessing and logistics. I started as an intern in geoprocessing, creating thematic maps, adjusting cartographic databases, and collecting geospatial data. Over time, I progressed to the role of Senior Consultant in Logistics and Geoprocessing, participating in complex projects involving route optimisation and strategic territory planning. I worked with clients from different sectors, such as insurance, telecommunications, and retail, helping them implement solutions that improved logistics and delivery scheduling. This experience gave me valuable insights into the use of geotechnology and introduced me to the concept of product management.

## Experience at Maplink
Afterwards, I worked at **Maplink** for about 3 years, starting as a Presales Specialist and later taking on the role of Project Manager. There, I led a team of specialists in presales and implementation, focusing on route optimisation solutions and APIs. This position allowed me to deeply understand client needs and work closely with Product Owners (POs) to develop customised solutions that met the operational requirements of our partners. As Project Manager, I was responsible for implementing technologies that optimised route processes, increasing operational efficiency for clients.

## Experience at iFood
These years at Geograph and Maplink were crucial in shaping my career and preparing me for bigger challenges at **iFood**. At iFood, I was able to apply and expand my knowledge in products, leading the creation of a mapping platform for logistics management and expansion. This initiative was a milestone in my career, as we managed to increase logistical capacity by 400% and reduce operational costs by 95%. The platform automated processes, reducing the expansion time from 90 days to just one day. Furthermore, we used remote sensing technologies and artificial intelligence to identify high-demand areas, which was key to the success of the expansion.

At iFood, I worked on several fronts. Initially, I led the Location Tribe, responsible for the company's geospatial platform. Then, I took on leadership roles in other teams, including the Delivery team, which manages the entire lifecycle of a route, from offering it to the couriers to completion, as well as being responsible for customer support. Later, I managed the app platform teams, communication, and interactions with couriers. These teams faced challenges such as ensuring KYC (Know Your Customer), facial biometrics, and the implementation of a platform to identify fraudulent behaviour in real-time, applying restrictions to couriers with inadequate conduct.

I also participated in launching a new version of the courier app, overseeing all project stages to ensure that the product met user expectations and was scalable. I have always been involved in full-cycle projects, from conception to implementation, seeking to ensure that the delivered solutions were robust and met market needs.

## Leadership Approach and Results
My leadership approach focuses on motivation and collaboration. During the development of the mapping platform, I needed to gain stakeholders' trust regarding the accuracy of the results. To do this, I conducted alignment meetings and proof of concept, ensuring active stakeholder participation in strategic decisions. This approach helped build the project's credibility and demonstrated the importance of teamwork and open communication.

Throughout my career, I have always placed great importance on measurable results. At iFood, we developed a return route that reduced financial losses by 20% for the most common type of courier fraud, while also improving operational predictability. On the mapping platform, we reduced the logistics expansion time by 98% and operational costs by 95%. Additionally, we developed a new way of creating service areas for restaurants, using real distances, which resulted in a significant increase in iFood's operational margin, generating an additional R\$3.6 million per month.




## Alignment with Manual.co's Mission
I am motivated to work at Manual.co because I believe my experience in logistics and digital product development can contribute to the company's mission of extending healthy years of life for people. I identify with Manual.co's values, such as the focus on continuous improvement and patient-centred care. I would like to understand more about how Manual.co fosters innovation among its teams and learn more about the main challenges faced by the product team. I am confident that my experience can add value, and I am excited to be part of this journey.
